Romeo and Juliet

Act 2, Scene 2

The Famous Balcony Scene

Romeo: But, soft! what light through yonder window breaks? It is the east, and Juliet is the sun.

Juliet: O Romeo, Romeo! wherefore art thou Romeo? Deny thy father and refuse thy name.

Romeo: Shall I hear more, or shall I speak at this?

Juliet: 'Tis but thy name that is my enemy; Thou art thyself, though not a Montague.

Romeo: With love's light wings did I o'er-perch these walls; For stony limits cannot hold love out.

Juliet: If they do see thee, they will murder thee.

Romeo: I have night's cloak to hide me from their sight; And but thou love me, let them find me here.

Juliet: Dost thou love me? I know thou wilt say "Ay", And I will take thy word.
